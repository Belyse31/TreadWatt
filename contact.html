FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does a TreadWatt installation cost?

Costs vary based on scale and location. A single tile prototype costs $200-320. For commercial installations (100+ tiles), we offer volume discounts and financing options. Contact us for a detailed quote.

Can I build my own TreadWatt system?

Absolutely! We provide open-source build guides, schematics, and code. We encourage DIY builders and offer technical support.

What's the expected lifespan of the system?

TreadWatt tiles are designed for 20+ years of operation with minimal maintenance. Piezoelectric materials are solid-state and highly durable. Battery packs may need replacement every 5-7 years depending on usage.

Is TreadWatt suitable for outdoor use?

Yes! Our tiles are IP65 rated (dust and water resistant) and tested for temperatures from -20¬∞C to 60¬∞C. They're designed for sidewalks, transit stations, and outdoor public spaces.

How much energy does one tile generate?

A single tile generates 5-8 joules per step. In moderate traffic (50 steps/minute), this translates to 2-5 watts continuous power, or approximately 2.4 kWh per day.

Do you offer installation services?

We work with certified installation partners in select regions. For large deployments, we provide on-site training and technical support. Contact us to discuss your project needs.

Can TreadWatt integrate with existing building systems?

Yes! Our systems can feed power directly to building loads, charge battery banks, or connect to the grid through inverters. We provide IoT integration for smart building management.

Are there grants or incentives available?

Many regions offer renewable energy grants, tax credits, and green building incentives. We can help you identify applicable programs and assist with grant applications.
