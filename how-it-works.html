Core Technology

Piezoelectric Materials

Piezoelectricity is the ability of certain materials to generate an electric charge in response to applied mechanical stress. TreadWatt leverages this phenomenon using advanced ceramic and polymer composites.

Materials We Use

üî∏ PZT Ceramics (Lead Zirconate Titanate)

  • Efficiency: High piezoelectric coefficient (d33 = 300-600 pC/N)
  • Durability: Excellent for high-stress applications
  • Use Case: Primary energy generation in floor tiles

üî∏ PVDF Polymers (Polyvinylidene Fluoride)

  • Flexibility: Bendable and lightweight
  • Sensitivity: Responds to low-force impacts
  • Use Case: Secondary layers and gym equipment sensors

üî∏ Composite Arrays

  • Innovation: Multi-layer stacks for enhanced output
  • Optimization: Tuned resonance frequencies
  • Use Case: Maximizing energy capture per step
